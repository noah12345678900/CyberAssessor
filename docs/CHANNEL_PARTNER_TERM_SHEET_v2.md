### 1. Nature of the Product (read first — drives everything below)
- The Product is **Commercial Computer Software**, developed exclusively at Owner's private expense, delivered as a **locally-installed desktop application** (Windows installer). It is **not** a hosted/SaaS service.
- Owner does **not** host, store, process, or transmit customer data or Controlled Unclassified Information (CUI) on Owner-controlled infrastructure. All source artifacts remain on the end user's machine.
- The end user is solely responsible for routing any LLM/API traffic through an endpoint authorized for their data classification (e.g., AWS Bedrock GovCloud, Azure OpenAI Gov) when CUI or DoD-IL data is in scope.

### 2. Relationship & Scope
- Partner is appointed as a **non-exclusive** partner for the Product.
- Scope is limited to the specific **agencies, programs, or contract vehicles** listed in Exhibit A (not vague account names — e.g., "PEO XYZ under Contract #___," not "the Navy").
- Owner retains the unrestricted right to sell directly and through other partners, including any account, agency, or vehicle not in Exhibit A.

### 3. Partner Role (check one — drives the rate)
- [ ] **Referral:** Partner introduces; Owner closes, contracts, licenses, supports.
- [ ] **Reseller:** Partner sells, invoices, provides tier-1 support; Owner provides Product + tier-2.
- [ ] **Vehicle holder:** Partner places the Product on its own contract vehicle (GSA Schedule, IDIQ, etc.) and holds the government paper.

### 4. Revenue Share (GovCon-realistic ranges)
- Referral: **5–10%** of net license revenue.
- Reseller (active tier-1 support + integration): **15–25%**.
- Vehicle holder pushing paper only: **5–15%**, typically **flat across years** (see note).
- Partner share: **____%**, applied per Exhibit A account.
- **Net revenue** = collected license/subscription fees, **less** refunds, taxes, third-party pass-through costs, **and any government contract access fees** (e.g., GSA Industrial Funding Fee ~0.75%, vehicle surcharges).
- *Step-down note:* A declining renewal share (e.g., yr-1 higher, yr-2+ lower) applies **only** where Owner holds the customer relationship. Where Partner holds the contract vehicle, renewals flow through Partner's paper and the share is **flat**.

### 5. Pricing & Compliance Protection
- Owner sets list pricing. Partner may not sell below the **floor price** (Exhibit B) without written approval.
- Partner must obtain Owner's **written approval of the final price offered to any government buyer**, to protect Owner's commercial pricing baseline (e.g., against GSA Most-Favored-Customer / CSP / TDR exposure).
- No bundling, sublicensing, repackaging, or white-labeling without Owner's prior written consent.

### 6. Intellectual Property & Government Data Rights
- All IP, source code, and the right to modify the Product remain **exclusively owned by Owner**. Partner receives a limited, revocable, non-transferable right to **market and resell licenses only** — no access to or transfer of source code or technical data.
- Government rights in the Product are governed **solely by Owner's standard Commercial EULA**, consistent with **FAR 12.212** and **DFARS 227.7202** (Commercial Computer Software / Commercial Computer Software Documentation).
- Partner shall **not** accept, agree to, or flow down any **noncommercial** data-rights clauses on Owner's behalf — specifically including **DFARS 252.227-7013 / -7014** — without Owner's prior written consent.
- Partner may flow down only those **FAR/DFARS clauses mandatory for commercial items** (e.g., FAR 52.244-6). Any additional flow-down requires Owner's written consent.

### 7. End-User License & Security Responsibility
- Every sale is contingent on the government/end-user accepting **Owner's standard Commercial EULA**. Partner may not grant Product access to any end user who has not accepted it.
- Because the Product runs locally and Owner hosts no customer data, the **end user (and, where applicable, Partner)** is responsible for: deploying the Product within their authorized boundary, configuring an authorized LLM endpoint for CUI/IL data, and meeting their own DFARS 252.204-7012 / FedRAMP obligations. **Owner provides the Product "as-is" with respect to the customer's environment and endpoint authorization.**

### 8. Support & Delivery
- Support responsibilities split per the role selected in §3.
- Owner provides reasonable Product updates and security patches during the term.

### 9. Term & Termination
- Initial term: **12 months**, auto-renewing in 12-month periods unless either party gives **60 days'** notice.
- Either party may terminate for material breach uncured after **30 days'** written notice.
- **On termination — no contract transfer.** Government contracts cannot be assigned from Partner to Owner without a formal Novation (Anti-Assignment Act, 41 U.S.C. § 6305). Instead: Partner stops all new marketing/sales; Partner may **fulfill the base term of active task orders already in place** ("run-out period") but **forfeits option years and renewals**; earned commissions on already-closed deals survive.

### 10. Confidentiality & Liability
- Mutual NDA covers pricing, customer lists, and non-public Product information.
- Liability is capped at fees paid under this agreement in the preceding 12 months; neither party is liable for consequential damages. (Final figures for definitive agreement.)

### 11. Non-Binding
- This term sheet is for discussion only and creates no obligation until a definitive written agreement is signed by both parties.
